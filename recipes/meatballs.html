Meatballs

Flavorful and tender, this classic weeknight meal comes together in under an hour and leaves you with 16 delicious meatballs.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 85/15 lean ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/3 cup panko (Japanese-style breadcrumbs)
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • 2 large garlic cloves, grated (about 1 tsp.)
  • 2 large garlic cloves, grated (about 1 tsp.)
  • 2 large garlic cloves, grated (about 1 tsp.)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 (24-oz.) jar marinara sauce
  • 12 ounces uncooked spaghetti, broken in half

Instructions

  1. Stir together beef, pork, eggs, parsley, Parmesan, panko, milk, garlic, and 1 1/2 teaspoons of the salt until combined. Shape mixture into 16 balls (about 3 tablespoons each).
  2. Select SAUTÃ‰ setting on a programmable pressure multicooker (such as Instant Pot). Add oil to cooker. Select HIGH temperature setting, and allow to preheat, 2 to 3 minutes. Working in 2 batches, add meatballs to cooker. Cook, turning occasionally, until meatballs are browned, 6 to 8 minutes. Transfer cooked meatballs to a plate. Press CANCEL, and stir in 3 cups water and remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt, scraping to loosen browned bits on bottom of cooker.
  3. Add marinara sauce, spaghetti, and meatballs to cooker, stirring gently to combine. Cover cooker with lid, and lock in place. Turn steam release handle to SEALING position. Select MANUAL/PRESSURE COOK setting. Select HIGH pressure for 8 minutes. (It will take 14 to 15 minutes for cooker to come up to pressure before cooking begins.)
  4. When cooking is finished, carefully turn steam release handle to VENTING position, and let steam fully escape (float valve will drop). (This will take 6 to 8 minutes.) Remove lid from cooker.
  5. Stir spaghetti to unstick any noodles. Let stand 5 minutes. Serve with additional grated Parmesan.
Enjoy!
